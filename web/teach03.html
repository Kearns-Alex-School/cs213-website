Instructions

Academic by Daniel Van Orman

Formatted like a scholarly book or article. Titles appear similar to common formatting in academic texts - the main title is large and bold like a chapter title, headings (h3) are left-aligned, all caps, and bold like subtitles, and subheadings are italicized like minor sections. The monochromatic color scheme gives the website a more constant/predictable feel (not flashy or trying to grab attention) and the font size is small, but not too small. Copy protection and Roman numerals for lists contribute to an intellectual atmosphere.

Easy to Read by Daniel Van Orman

Optimized for high readability. The font size is large enough to make text more visible but not too large as to create too much whitespace. The font family is one of the easiest to read and text width is limited to a vertical column to avoid strain and increase reading space by lessening how much one's eyes must shift horizontally. Text and background colors have a high contrast (black on white, white on red, etc.) to improve visibility. Text is left-aligned to reduce searching for text and to create a familiar pattern with a straight edge on one side (ex: reading center-aligned, with two non-straight edges, is more difficult than left-aligned).

Programming Text Editor by Daniel Van Orman

Modeled after a basic text editor for programming languages. The main title is similar to the top of a window, except for its boldness (to help it stand out as a title) where as a file path would not be bold. Below it is the menu, where "File", "Edit", and other options would be. The text file begins with a list made to look like "#include" or "import" statements. Headings (h3) are comments, but not indented to improve visual appearance. General text is green, a stereotype for code, and subheadings and links are colored similarly to keywords. The bottom of the webpage looks like the bottom row of a text editor, which would normally have statistics and settings information.

The Unexpected by Alex Kearns

Modeled after nothing really. As the title suggests, this is a style that does not fit in with the others and as such, should be unexpected. Even with such a dramatic change of style, color, and separation each of these individual elements come together in a beautiful style. Headings are colored banners with more urgent ones standing out more. With each paragraph tag grouped with its color paired with margins, this relatively simple combination is very popping to the eye while making it unexpected compared to the other styles. Sometimes, a change in setting can be nice, warm, and relaxing.
